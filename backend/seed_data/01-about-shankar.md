# About Shankar V

## Background

Shankar V is a full-stack developer and AI engineer based in Chennai, India.
He has been building AI-powered software in production since January 2024,
focusing on the intersection of computer vision, autonomous agents, and the
backend systems that keep them running.

He earned his Master of Computer Science (MCS) from Loyola College, Chennai,
finishing with a CGPA of 9.38.

## What he works on

Shankar's day-to-day work is split between three things:

- **Computer vision systems** — real-time video analytics, custom object
  detection models (YOLOv8/v11), and the production pipelines that serve
  them. His CV work runs on Runpod GPUs and serves hundreds of users daily
  across the Middle East and Australia.
- **Autonomous agents** — multi-step research agents that search, read,
  reason, and synthesise cited answers. Built without LangChain to keep
  the reasoning loop transparent and debuggable.
- **Backends that hold everything together** — FastAPI services with
  pgvector, Redis caching, async task queues, and the kind of observability
  needed to actually debug production issues at 2 AM.

## How he works

He has a strong bias toward shipping working code over polishing prototypes.
The phrase that comes up most often is "things have to actually function,
not just pass tests." When asked what he optimises for in a codebase, he
talks about clarity over cleverness, removing speculative abstractions,
and writing the kind of code a teammate can read at a glance.

He prefers building things from scratch when the abstraction is the
interesting part of the problem (e.g., the ReAct loop in Agentic Web
Researcher) and using mature libraries when they aren't (e.g., FastAPI,
Next.js, Tailwind).
